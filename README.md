# AI-Native Organizations

A research project exploring how AI changes organizations—not only software development.

Most discussions about AI focus on models, agents, and individual productivity. This repository focuses on a different question:

> What happens to the organization once AI becomes abundant?

## Core hypothesis

AI shifts the primary bottleneck from coding and execution to organizational systems: prioritization, context, coordination, decision-making, governance, and leadership.

As execution becomes cheaper and faster, the quality of the surrounding organization matters more.

## Research questions

- What changes inside organizations after AI becomes a baseline capability?
- Which management practices stop working?
- How do planning, hiring, budgeting, and performance management evolve?
- What becomes the new constraint when implementation capacity increases?
- How should product and engineering organizations be redesigned?
- What is the role of managers in an AI-native operating model?

## Method

This is an evidence-driven project. Claims should be grounded in primary or well-verified sources such as:

- CEO or leadership memos
- Earnings calls
- Official engineering and company blogs
- Public interviews and conference talks
- Company policies and job descriptions
- Academic or industry research
- Verified internal documents reported by credible publications

The goal is not to collect AI announcements. It is to identify concrete changes to organizational behavior and operating models.
